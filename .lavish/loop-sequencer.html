A loop-friendly sequencer

Today the Sequencer is a single 16-step pattern that loops on itself. This explores turning it into a compositional surface — layer loops, reuse them as blocks, grow past 16 steps into whole songs, and keep a library of beats you can fire alongside live play.

Why it's good
  • Layering is the literal mental model — stack lanes, mute/solo to arrange on the fly.
  • "Record lane from live play" ties straight into the existing performance engine.

Costs

  • Song-length structure still implicit until blocks are added.

Today's Sequencer — three things you flagged

Before new structure, the current screen has fixable problems. These fold straight into the redesign — the freed left half plus the wasted margins are exactly the room the cramped parts need.

REAL ESTATE
Wasted top & bottom margins
On your 12 Pro Max the grid floats in the middle with dead space above and below. → Fix: let the sequencer fill the full height; the new lanes / pages expand into that room instead of leaving it empty.
FEELS DEAD
Gate slider seems to do nothing
It is wired — it sets note-off timing inside each 1/16 step — but a pad's release tail masks it, so it's inaudible. → Fix: make it perceptible: crisp staccato at low values, legato / tied notes near 100%.
CRAMPED
"Step N" editor is squeezed
Degree picker, D-pad, rest, and gate are packed into a tiny strip. → Fix: move the step editor into the freed left half — full-height, breathing room, the D-pad at a usable size.
