ring blooms under your thumb · drag angle → root · push out past the ring → minor · release to set
▸ press & hold the KEY button, drag, release
Hold KEY → a 12-root ring blooms under the thumb. Angle picks the root; push out past the ring into the outer band to make it minor — no separate toggle, the fingertip never leaves the wheel; release commits. Mirrors the Joystick/Ring language. Full scale list + octave stay in the tap-to-open sheet. This is exactly how the Swift build already behaves.
Alt A2 · Swipe keyboard
▲ PUSH UP = MINOR
▼ PUSH DOWN = MINOR
C Major — swipe to a key, push out for minor
Interactive now — hold KEY → a 2×6 chromatic grid; slide to a key and push out past the top or bottom edge to minor-ize it (up for the top row, down for the bottom). Familiar and cheap to read exact letters; the trade-off vs. the wheel is a denser target grid. Being built in Swift alongside A1 so you can A/B them on the phone.
Alt A3 · XY glide pad
SCALE ↑
ROOT →
G Major — drag the puck
One square trackpad: horizontal = root, vertical = scale (so you get any scale in one gesture, not just Major/Minor). Most expressive and scales to all 10 scales — but least precise for a specific key, and a beginner may not build a mental map of the 2-D grid quickly. Good “power user” complement to A1.
All three keep the current KEY sheet reachable by a normal tap (octave, full 10-scale list, exotic roots). The press-and-hold gesture is purely an accelerator for the common case: “get me to a new key/major-minor fast, mid-performance.”
